ASSUMPTIONS USED
---
- The resolution-owner tag vocabulary is treated as fixed for v1: [REQUIRES REG D COUNSEL INPUT],
  [REQUIRES SECURITIZE CONFIRMATION], [REQUIRES SECOND STREET INPUT], [REQUIRES INTERNAL DECISION].
  New tags may be added if a new named party enters scope — this is tracked as UI-P10-001.

- The nine-category classification model is treated as exhaustive for v1 project documentation.
  The working assumption is that no new category type will be needed. If a new type emerges,
  it must be added via internal decision (UI-P10-001).

- The 8-status document status model is an extension of the STATUS-BOARD model. The STATUS-BOARD
  carries the standard 5 statuses (SHELL CREATED, DRAFTED, READY, APPROVED, LOCKED). The
  P1-010 policy adds 3 operational sub-statuses (PARTIAL/BLOCKED, WAITING ON LEGAL,
  WAITING ON VENDOR) for internal tracking purposes. These are compatible but the STATUS-BOARD
  itself uses only the standard 5. This is an internal operational distinction — not a conflict.

- The five certainty levels (LOCKED TRUTH through UNRESOLVED DEPENDENCY) are treated as a
  complete hierarchy for v1. Sub-levels (e.g., distinguishing between "assumption confirmed
  verbally but not in writing" vs. "assumption unconfirmed") are not introduced for v1 to
  avoid classification overhead.

- All examples cited in Section 7 (Definitions) are drawn from existing unresolved items in
  prior Phase 1 documents. No invented examples were used.

- The carry-forward model in Section 12 does not specify a technical mechanism for tracking
  (e.g., a dedicated tracking spreadsheet or issue tracker). The working assumption is that
  the open-items companion files and STATUS-BOARD together provide sufficient tracking for v1.
  If a formal tracking system is adopted, this section will need to be updated.

- The document treats itself as subject to the policy it defines. Section 16 (Review Notes)
  makes this explicit. This is intentional — the policy must be credible and self-applicable.

CLASSIFICATION MODEL CONSTRUCTION
---
The nine categories were derived by examining all prior unresolved items across P1-002 through
P1-009 and extracting the distinct resolution patterns:

1. Items resolved by formal change control → Locked Decision
2. Items with structural lock but pending detail → Conditionally Locked Decision
3. Items provisionally resolved by author → Working Assumption
4. Items not yet assigned → Open Unknown
5. Items requiring legal input → External Legal Dependency
6. Items requiring vendor input → Vendor Confirmation Dependency
7. Items requiring named internal stakeholder → Internal Decision Dependency
8. Items that can be tracked without blocking work → Non-Blocking Unresolved Item
9. Items that prevent work from proceeding → Blocking Unresolved Item

Categories 8 and 9 are cross-cutting (any item of types 1-7 can also be blocking or non-blocking).
The model treats blocking/non-blocking as a status attribute of unresolved items, not a
separate category type.

PROHIBITED BEHAVIORS LIST CONSTRUCTION
---
The eleven prohibited behaviors in Section 13 were derived from patterns observed in
documentation failures in regulated-domain projects:
- Certainty upgrade without evidence: the most common drafting failure
- TBD without classification: leaves readers unable to act on the item
- Treating absence of challenge as confirmation: particularly risky in review cycles
- Inferring legal finality: specific risk given Flamingo's Reg D context
- Inferring vendor behavior from documentation: specific risk given Securitize integration
- Removing items without resolving them: silently drops accountability
- False blocking/non-blocking classification: corrupts status tracking
- Unilateral internal decisions: bypasses named stakeholder accountability
- Architectural workarounds for unresolved dependencies: masks risk
- Assumption inheritance across documents: working assumption becomes invisible fact
