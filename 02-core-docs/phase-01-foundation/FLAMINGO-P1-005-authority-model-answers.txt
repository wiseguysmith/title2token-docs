ASSUMPTIONS USED
---
- Authority is organized into six typed categories: legal, operational, technical, administrative,
  review, and record. This taxonomy was selected to make the authority model precise and
  implementation-actionable. No new authority types were invented.

- "Record authority" is split into legal and operational because the distinction is structurally
  critical: the TA holds legal record authority; Flamingo holds operational record authority.
  These must never be described as equivalent.

- The "three authority planes" framing (legal, operational, technical) was used to explain the
  non-collapsibility of completion states in accessible terms.

- The Compliance / Review Operator is treated as the sole holder of "review authority" within the
  UNDER_REVIEW state. The Platform Administrator may have escalated review authority pending CLD-003.

- Blockchain execution layer is treated as holding "technical authority" in the limited sense of
  executing on-chain instructions. It holds no authority in the legal or operational sense.

- Legal Counsel is included as an authority subject only to confirm it holds no platform authority.
  Its advisory role is external and does not constitute platform or legal record authority.

- Broker-dealer, ATS, and custodian are out of scope for v1 and receive no authority assignments.

- Reconciliation authority is flagged as unresolved (UI-P5-004) because LD-017 locks the
  discrepancy resolution rule (TA supersedes) but does not define who initiates reconciliation
  or what the operational process is.

---

SELECTED AUTHORITY CATEGORIES
---
- Legal authority: binding power over securities, ownership, investor rights
- Operational authority: power to act within Flamingo platform workflow
- Technical authority: power to execute system-level actions (on-chain, API)
- Administrative authority: power to configure and manage platform
- Review authority: power to evaluate and approve/reject within a defined state
- Record authority: power to write to an authoritative record (legal or operational)

---

SELECTED AUTHORITY PRINCIPLES
---
AP-001: Platform permission ≠ legal authority
AP-002: Chain execution ≠ legal completion
AP-003: Operational tracking ≠ legal recordation
AP-004: Review approval ≠ TA-recorded completion
AP-005: Legal holder-of-record authority is external to Flamingo
AP-006: Flamingo cannot directly alter TA legal records
AP-007: Blockchain execution carries no legal discretion
AP-008: Authority does not transfer by proximity
AP-009: Legal completion requires TA recordation
AP-010: TA acknowledgment ≠ TA recordation
