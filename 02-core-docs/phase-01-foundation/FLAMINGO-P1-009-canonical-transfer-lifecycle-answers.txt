ASSUMPTIONS USED
---
- The 8-state chain is treated as strictly sequential with no sub-states in the canonical path.
  Exception outcomes (rejection, failure, mismatch) are adjacent, not canonical.

- "Entered when" and "Advanced when" are treated as distinct: entry is the trigger that
  moves the transfer INTO the state; advancement is the condition that moves it OUT.

- Four completion layers are used: no completion, technical execution, TA recordation, and legal
  completion. Handoff and acknowledgment are not classified as completion layers — they are
  intermediate operational events within the TA handoff domain.

- LEGALLY_COMPLETE is treated as Flamingo's recognition of a TA legal act, not a Flamingo
  legal determination. This framing preserves the authority boundary while explaining the state.

- Exception paths are explicitly non-canonical. No REJECTED, FAILED, or ERROR states are added
  to the canonical sequence. These are operational exception handling concerns for Phase 3.

- The "divergence window" (between CHAIN_EXECUTED and TA_RECORDED) is acknowledged implicitly
  in the state definitions — on-chain state may not match TA-recorded state during this window.
  This aligns with P1-008 SOT matrix treatment.

- TA_INSTRUCTION_SENT is treated as "handoff initiation" — not a completion state in any sense.
  A successful API transmission is not the same as TA acknowledgment.

- Evidence standards table (Section 9) establishes the minimum evidence required to enter each
  state. This is intended to be implementation-constraining: any system that advances state
  without meeting the evidence minimum has a defect.

---

STATE DEFINITIONS USED
---
1. REQUESTED — receipt only; no review, approval, or execution
2. UNDER_REVIEW — manual compliance review in progress; no approval
3. APPROVED — cleared for execution; not executed
4. CHAIN_EXECUTED — on-chain execution confirmed; not legally complete
5. TA_INSTRUCTION_SENT — instruction transmitted; not acknowledged
6. TA_ACKNOWLEDGED — receipt confirmed by TA; not recorded
7. TA_RECORDED — TA has legally recorded; legal act complete
8. LEGALLY_COMPLETE — Flamingo recognizes confirmed TA_RECORDED; terminal

---

ADVANCEMENT PRINCIPLES USED
---
- No state skipping
- No out-of-sequence transitions
- External confirmations must be received signals, not inferred
- Investor action alone insufficient for any transition
- Admin review required for all v1 transfers
- LEGALLY_COMPLETE requires confirmed TA_RECORDED — no exceptions

---

EXCEPTION CATEGORIES NOTED
---
- Review rejection (at UNDER_REVIEW)
- Execution failure (at/before CHAIN_EXECUTED)
- TA instruction failure (at TA_INSTRUCTION_SENT — no acknowledgment)
- TA recording delay (at TA_ACKNOWLEDGED — no recording)
- Reconciliation mismatch (operational exception, any state)
- Stale / ambiguous status (operational exception)
