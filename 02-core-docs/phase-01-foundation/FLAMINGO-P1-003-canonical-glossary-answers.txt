KEY TERM DECISIONS
---

"Operational Registry" vs "Flamingo database":
Decision: "Operational Registry" is the canonical term. "Flamingo operational database" is acceptable shorthand in internal technical contexts only. The note is included in the Operational Registry definition itself to prevent drift.

"TA-Acknowledged Completion":
Decision: This is explicitly NOT a recognized completion boundary. The definition states this clearly. This prevents any implementation team from treating TA_ACKNOWLEDGED as a completion state.

"REDEEMED":
Decision: Defined as a token redemption event that is explicitly not equivalent to LEGALLY_COMPLETE. LD-042 is cited. The definition does not describe REDEEMED as a lifecycle state (because it is not one of the 8 canonical states) — it describes what it is and what it is not.

"Compliance and Review Operator":
Decision: Defined as a secondary designation for the Flamingo role in transfer review context. Clearly scoped to operational function only — not legal compliance responsibility.

"Forced Transfer":
Decision: Defined as subject to the same admin-review and lifecycle requirements as voluntary transfers. This prevents any implementation assumption that forced transfers bypass the 8-state chain.

Resolution-Owner Tags:
Decision: All 5 canonical tags are listed under the "Resolution-Owner Tag" term definition. This makes the glossary the single authoritative source for which tags are valid.

ASSUMPTIONS USED
---
- "Legal boundary" framing: definitions that touch legal questions are framed as how Flamingo uses the term operationally, not as legal opinions. This is consistent with P1-002 approach.
- "Securitize" is named in the Transfer Agent and Legal Holder of Record definitions because it is the designated TA for v1 per project constraints. This is structural, not an endorsement.
- "Second Street Capital" is named as the Launch Tenant because it is the designated v1 tenant per project constraints.
- "Multi-Tenant Architecture" is defined at the structural level only — the full tenant isolation model is Phase 2 scope (P2-002, P2-003).
- KYC and AML are defined structurally (what they are, that they are offchain) without specifying vendor, since vendor is open (UI-008).

PROHIBITED TERM DECISIONS
---
13 prohibited terms defined. Key rationale for non-obvious ones:
- "Flamingo's records" without "operational": omission of "operational" qualifier implies legal authority Flamingo does not hold. Even in internal technical documents, the qualifier should be present in any context where legal authority is in scope.
- "Transfer is complete" without state qualifier: ambiguity is the problem — this phrase could mean any of 3 different completion boundaries. Prohibited to force precision.
- "Securitize confirms the transfer": dangerous because TA_ACKNOWLEDGED (acknowledgment) is not TA_RECORDED (recording). The prohibited entry targets the specific misuse — using "confirms" when meaning "acknowledged receipt," not "recorded in books."
- "Blockchain confirms the transfer": same completion boundary confusion — on-chain execution is technical completion only.
