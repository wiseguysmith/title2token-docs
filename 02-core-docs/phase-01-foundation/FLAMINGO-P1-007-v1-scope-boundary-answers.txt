ASSUMPTIONS USED
---
- P1-006 System Context was a shell with no content. Scope document was drafted without relying
  on it. Any inconsistency must be resolved when P1-006 is drafted.

- "v1 is conservative by design" is used as the governing scope philosophy. When in doubt,
  scope is excluded, not included.

- In-scope capabilities were derived from: the 8-state transfer chain (LD-021), the admin-reviewed
  transfer requirement (LD-023, LD-029), the operational registry model (LD-015), and the
  blockchain usage model (LD-018).

- Out-of-scope items were derived from: identity locks (LD-001–LD-007) and v1 operating model
  locks (LD-029–LD-034).

- "Deferred" items are planning artifacts only — no commitment implied. They are listed because
  omitting them creates a vacuum that invites scope confusion.

- Investor accreditation verification is treated as external but adjacent (performed by issuer /
  counsel / third-party verifier). Flamingo may store accreditation data as an operational record.
  If a third-party verifier is integrated, a scope addendum is required (UI-P7-005).

- The "governing constraint" framing — any function that would make Flamingo a regulated substitute
  for another actor is out of scope — is used consistently because it gives a decision rule, not
  just a list.

---

SELECTED SCOPE INCLUSIONS
---
- Offering setup and onboarding support (admin-configured)
- Operational workflow orchestration (8-state chain)
- Compliance-aware review support (manual, tooling only)
- Admin-reviewed transfer handling (all v1 transfers)
- Operational registry maintenance (not legal books and records)
- Blockchain token representation and transfer restriction support
- Event logging and audit trail support
- TA instruction handoff and status tracking
- Reconciliation support (detection and flagging; resolution per LD-017)

---

SELECTED SCOPE EXCLUSIONS
---
- Issuer substitution / SPV structuring
- Legal advice or legal opinion
- TA legal recordkeeping or direct record mutation
- Broker-dealer functions
- ATS / secondary market
- Custody
- Self-serve investor transfer finalization
- Automated compliance screening
- Asserting legal finality from chain execution alone
- Real-time settlement
- Investor-facing trading UI
- Cross-border / multi-jurisdiction compliance logic

DEFERRED ITEMS
---
- Automated compliance screening
- Self-service investor transfer initiation
- Secondary market / ATS integration
- Real-time settlement
- Custodian integration
- Broker-dealer workflow integration
- Automated TA reconciliation
- Investor-facing transfer portal with direct execution
- Cross-border multi-jurisdiction support
- Expanded offering types beyond Reg D private placements
