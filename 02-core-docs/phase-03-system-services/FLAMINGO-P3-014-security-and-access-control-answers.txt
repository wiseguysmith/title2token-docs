FLAMINGO-P3-014 — Security and Access Control: Wallet and Permissions Service
ANSWERS FILE — Source: PHASE 3 LOCKED ANSWERS SOURCE BLOCK DOC 14

Q: Wallet count at MVP?
A: One primary wallet per investor/entity. Optional secondary by Compliance Officer approval only.

Q: Can one wallet map to more than one investor/entity?
A: No. Strict uniqueness constraint enforced at data and service layer.

Q: Internal wallet states?
A: SUBMITTED, UNDER_REVIEW, APPROVED, REJECTED, FROZEN, REPLACEMENT_PENDING, REVOKED

Q: What blocks during REPLACEMENT_PENDING?
A: All material actions (transfers, subscriptions) referencing the original wallet.

Q: What happens to the old wallet after replacement is approved?
A: Immediately moved to REVOKED. No simultaneous-APPROVED overlap at MVP.

Q: When is Securitize notified of wallet changes?
A: Only after replacement wallet reaches APPROVED state. Never during review.

Q: Emergency freeze triggers?
A: Lost access, suspected compromise, sanctions flag, legal hold.

Q: Who can apply an emergency freeze?
A: Compliance Officer or Platform Admin.

Q: RBAC for material wallet actions?
A: Tenant Admin: submit wallet, initiate replacement.
   Compliance Officer: approve, reject, authorize replacement, freeze, unfreeze.
   Platform Admin: emergency freeze, revoke.

Q: How does allowlist sync work?
A: Wallet Service emits canonical wallet change event.
   Blockchain Bridge consumes event and writes allowlist update to ERC-3643 on Base.
   Wallet Service does not interact directly with the blockchain.

Q: User-facing wallet status labels?
A: SUBMITTED -> "Submitted"
   UNDER_REVIEW -> "Under review"
   APPROVED -> "Approved"
   REJECTED -> "Action required"
   FROZEN -> "Frozen"
   REPLACEMENT_PENDING -> "Replacement in progress"
   REVOKED -> "Replaced"

Q: Authority when Flamingo allowlist and Base diverge?
A: Base is authoritative for enforcement (LD-041). Flamingo must investigate and resolve.

Q: Are revoked wallet records retained?
A: Yes. Material records per LD-051. Deletion not permitted.
