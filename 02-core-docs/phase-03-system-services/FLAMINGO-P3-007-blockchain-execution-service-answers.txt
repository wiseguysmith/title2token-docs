Q: Primary token standard for v1?
A: ERC-3643 on Base network (LD-035, LD-036).

Q: What is the Bridge's role?
A: Controlled orchestration layer for on-chain actions, event ingestion, and confirmation tracking.
   It does not generate TA instruction packets. It does not constitute legal authority.

Q: What on-chain actions does v1 support?
A: Mint, burn, freeze, unfreeze, allowlist updates, transfer-restriction-related writes.

Q: Application behavior model?
A: Asynchronous job/workflow-based. Callers do not block on on-chain confirmation.

Q: Transaction lifecycle stages?
A: TX_SUBMITTED -> pending confirmation -> TX_CONFIRMED -> EVENT_INGESTED -> handoff to downstream service.

Q: Delayed or failed confirmation handling?
A: Retry with configurable thresholds. Visible pending state. RECONCILIATION_SIGNAL_RAISED on escalation.

Q: TA packet model?
A: Bridge emits canonical execution result (TX_CONFIRMED). Downstream Transfer Orchestration
   advances state to CHAIN_EXECUTED. A separate downstream process generates the TA instruction packet.
   Bridge does not generate or send TA packets directly.

Q: TA instruction packet format?
A: UNRESOLVED. Must remain behind interface/config. [REQUIRES SECURITIZE CONFIRMATION] (CLD-006)

Q: RPC provider model?
A: Primary RPC provider + fallback provider abstraction. Provider-specific details are configurable.

Q: Gas strategy?
A: Simple app-controlled submission with configurable gas policy. No hardcoded values.

Q: Canonical Bridge event categories?
A: TX_SUBMITTED, TX_CONFIRMED, TX_FAILED, EVENT_INGESTED, ALLOWLIST_UPDATED,
   TOKENS_MINTED, TOKENS_BURNED, TOKENS_FROZEN, TOKENS_UNFROZEN,
   TRANSFER_WRITE_EXECUTED, RECONCILIATION_SIGNAL_RAISED

Q: What abstractions must remain behind interfaces?
A: TA instruction packet format, Securitize API specifics, exact holding-period rule encoding,
   some confirmation semantics, RPC provider identity.

Q: Does CHAIN_EXECUTED mean legal completion?
A: No. LD-026: on-chain execution does not constitute legal transfer completion.
