FLAMINGO-P2-011 Cap Table and Registry Boundary — Design Rationale

===================================================================
QUESTION 1: Why a three-layer model (Operational Registry / Base token evidence / TA legal records)?
===================================================================

Three distinct layers exist because three distinct authorities exist, each with its own scope:

1. Flamingo Operational Registry — authoritative for operational platform state (LD-013). Covers what Flamingo knows: workflow state, allocation decisions, transfer lifecycle positions, TA signal receipts. Its authority is operational, not legal.

2. Base / ERC-3643 token balance evidence — authoritative for on-chain state and transfer restriction enforcement (LD-041). Covers what actually exists on-chain: token balances, allowlist status, contract pause state. Its authority is technical, not legal.

3. Securitize / TA legal records — authoritative for legal holder-of-record determination (LD-011, LD-040). Covers who legally owns the security interest. Its authority is legal, not operational or technical.

Collapsing any two of these layers would create a false equivalence:
- Operational Registry ≠ legal record: LD-015 makes Flamingo's registry an operational copy
- Token balance ≠ legal ownership: CHAIN_EXECUTED ≠ LEGALLY_COMPLETE (P2-012)
- TA signals ≠ Flamingo operational triggers: TA signal receipt updates Flamingo state but is not the same as Flamingo making a legal determination

The three-layer model makes these distinctions explicit and citeable by later documents.

===================================================================
QUESTION 2: Why four certainty levels (allocated / minted / TA-acknowledged / TA-recorded)?
===================================================================

A binary "owned / not owned" display would be both inaccurate and dangerous. The actual operational journey involves four distinct confirmation moments, each with different legal weight:

1. Allocation (SS-003+): admin has made a platform-level decision. No tokens exist yet. Not a legal event.
2. Minting (SS-007 / CHAIN_EXECUTED): tokens exist on-chain. Technical event. Not a legal event.
3. TA-Acknowledged (TA_ACKNOWLEDGED): TA confirmed receipt of instruction. Acknowledgment of receipt only — not recordation.
4. TA-Recorded (TA_RECORDED): TA has recorded the transfer in legal books-and-records. The legally significant event.

Showing all four allows operators to understand the platform state accurately. Conflating them would cause:
- False legal certainty signals at the allocation or minting stage
- Confusion between TA_ACKNOWLEDGED and TA_RECORDED (a known failure pattern per P1-011 §7, item 6)
- Inability to identify divergence or lag between operational and legal state

===================================================================
QUESTION 3: Why is divergence treated as "expected" rather than as an error?
===================================================================

Workflow timing means divergence is structurally normal. After a TA instruction is sent, there is a latency period before the TA processes and signals back. During that window, the Flamingo Operational Registry shows TA_INSTRUCTION_SENT, but the TA's records haven't yet been updated to reflect the transfer. This is not an error — it is the normal in-flight state.

Treating all divergence as errors would flood the reconciliation queue with false positives and obscure actual discrepancies that require attention.

The model distinguishes:
- Expected lag: normal; display "pending TA confirmation"; no escalation yet
- Persistent lag beyond threshold: escalate to P2-014 reconciliation [pending Securitize confirmation of threshold]
- Detectable mismatch: conflicting values between layers; always escalate

===================================================================
QUESTION 4: Why is the conservative display posture (RC-010, DV-001) a standing rule rather than a context-specific one?
===================================================================

Because the consequences of displaying false legal certainty are more severe than the consequences of displaying conservative uncertainty.

If Flamingo displays "TA-Recorded" when it has only received TA_ACKNOWLEDGED, an investor or admin may act on that display as if the transfer is legally complete — and that assumption may be legally wrong.

If Flamingo displays "Pending TA confirmation" when the TA has already recorded, the consequence is a brief operational delay — easily corrected when the signal arrives.

The asymmetry in consequences supports a conservative default as the standing rule, not a context-dependent judgment call. This is consistent with P1-010 (Assumptions and Unknowns Policy) and P2-012 (completion boundary language rules).

===================================================================
QUESTION 5: Why are OR-001 through OR-008 defined as record categories rather than specific data objects?
===================================================================

This is a Phase 2 product/control document. Specific data object definitions (field names, types, schema) belong in Phase 3 (P3-003 Data Object Catalog). At this layer, naming the eight operational record categories serves two purposes:
1. Establishes the canonical vocabulary that P2-013 (audit events), P2-014 (reconciliation), and Phase 3 documents can reference
2. Defines what belongs in the Operational Registry without prescribing implementation-level schema decisions prematurely
